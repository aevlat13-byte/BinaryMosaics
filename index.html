What is a bitstream? ?

A bitstream is the binary data for every pixel in order. 1-bit uses 0/1 per pixel; 2-bit uses 00/01/10/11.

What is row-major order? ?

Read each row from left to right, then move to the next row top-to-bottom.

Why do 4 colours need 2 bits? ?

Two bits can represent four values: 00, 01, 10, and 11.

Decode Challenge Tutorial ?
  1. Read the legend and bitstream shown for the current level.
  2. Click Start levels to begin at Level 1 (easiest).
  3. Paint the blank grid by decoding the bits. The image reveals as you paint.
  4. Click each square to paint one cell at a time (no brush dragging).
  5. Press Check to test your decoded picture.
  6. If correct, the game automatically moves to the next level.
